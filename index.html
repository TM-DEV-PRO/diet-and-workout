Your Weekly Exercise Plan

This plan combines Cardio (for fat loss & heart health), Strength (to build muscle & prevent loose skin), and Flexibility. Start slowly and listen to your body.

Day 1: Cardio & Flexibility

1. Cardio (30-45 mins)

Brisk Walking, Cycling, or Cross-Trainer. Start at a pace you can maintain.

Why: Burns calories and strengthens your heart. Crucial for BP and cholesterol management.

2. Flexibility (10 mins)

Gentle full-body stretching. Hold each stretch for 20-30 seconds. Do not bounce.

Why: Reduces risk of injury, improves blood flow, and helps with muscle soreness.

Day 2: Strength & Flexibility

1. Strength Training (20-30 mins)

3 sets of 10-12 reps: Bodyweight Squats, Lunges (or half-lunges), Wall Push-ups (easier on joints), Plank (hold for 20-30 secs), Dumbbell Rows (use light dumbbells or water bottles).

Why: Builds muscle, which burns calories even at rest. This is KEY to preventing loose skin.

2. Flexibility (10 mins)

Full-body stretching. Focus on chest, back, and legs.

Day 3: Cardio & Flexibility

1. Cardio (35-40 mins)

Try to slightly increase your pace or duration from Day 1. Brisk Walking, Jogging (if comfortable), or Swimming.

Why: Builds cardiovascular endurance and burns overall body fat.

2. Flexibility (10 mins)

Gentle full-body stretching.

Day 4: Strength & Flexibility

1. Strength Training (20-30 mins)

3 sets of 10-12 reps: Repeat Day 2 exercises. Try to improve your form or increase reps slightly.

Why: Consistency builds muscle. This metabolic boost is essential for long-term weight loss.

2. Flexibility (10 mins)

Full-body stretching.

Day 5: Cardio & Flexibility

1. Cardio (40-45 mins)

Push yourself a little, but listen to your body. Brisk Walking, Cycling, or Swimming.

Why: Continues to improve heart health and burn calories effectively.

2. Flexibility (10 mins)

Gentle full-body stretching.

Day 6: Strength & Core

1. Strength Training (20-30 mins)

3 sets of 10-12 reps: Squats, Lunges, Wall Push-ups, Dumbbell Rows, Plank (try 2-3 sets).

2. Core Focus (5-10 mins)

Crunches (gentle), Leg Raises (modified), Bird-Dog. This helps strengthen the "over flat" area you mentioned.

Why: A strong core supports your back and improves posture, making your stomach appear flatter.

Day 7: Active Recovery or Rest

Rest or Light Activity

Listen to your body. You can take a full rest day, or go for a light 20-30 minute walk.

Why: Your muscles need time to repair and grow stronger. Rest is not lazy, it's productive!

Your Key Questions Answered ("The Why")

Understanding *why* you're doing something is key to success. Here are the principles behind your plan.

This is our #1 priority. The plan attacks this in three ways:

  • Low Sodium: By avoiding added salt, pickles, and processed foods, we directly reduce the strain on your blood vessels, helping lower BP.
  • High Fiber: Oats, dals, salads, and fruits contain soluble fiber, which acts like a sponge and helps *remove* bad cholesterol (LDL) from your body.
  • Healthy Fats & Exercise: Using less oil and choosing lean protein (chicken, fish, dal) reduces bad fat intake. Cardio exercise further improves your good cholesterol (HDL).

This is a valid concern. Your plan is specifically designed to minimize this risk:

  • Gradual Loss: Our calorie target (~1700-1800 kcal) aims for 0.5-1kg per week. Rapid loss doesn't give your skin time to adjust. Slow and steady is the key.
  • Strength Training: This is critical! By building lean muscle (with exercises like squats, lunges, push-ups), you are building a firm layer underneath the skin. This helps "fill out" the space as fat is lost.
  • Protein & Hydration: Eating adequate protein (dal, chicken, fish, paneer) provides the building blocks for collagen, which gives skin its elasticity. Staying well-hydrated also helps skin health.

This is a very common question! The short answer is: you cannot "spot reduce" fat. You can't choose *where* your body burns fat from. Doing 1000 crunches won't melt belly fat (it will just build muscle *under* the fat).

Here is the correct strategy, which this plan follows:

  • Lose Overall Fat: The Cardio in your plan, combined with the Calorie Deficit from your diet, will burn overall body fat. Your body will decide where to take it from (often the belly, or "over flat" area, is one of the last places, so be patient!).
  • Strengthen the Core: The Strength & Core exercises (like planks, crunches) will strengthen the abdominal muscles. As the fat layer on top reduces, these toned muscles will become visible and give you a flatter, stronger midsection.

Living in Bangalore means great food is everywhere! You don't have to be a hermit. Use these rules:

  • Grill > Gravy: Choose Tandoori chicken/fish/paneer over rich, creamy, high-sodium curries like Butter Chicken or Korma.
  • Ask for "No Butter/Ghee": When ordering rotis, ask for them dry. Ask for dal tadka with "no ghee" or "minimal oil".
  • Sides are Key: Order a large green salad or clear soup (like rasam or veg clear soup) as a starter to fill up.
  • Be Mindful of Millets: Bangalore has great millet options (like Ragi). A Ragi Mudde with Sambar is a fantastic, high-fiber lunch, but be mindful of the portion.

Early Morning (6:00 - 6:30 AM)

  • 1 glass of lukewarm water with half a lemon.
  • OR 1 glass of Jeera (cumin) water.
  • OR 1 glass of Fenugreek (methi) water.

Why: This kick-starts your metabolism, aids digestion, and helps to hydrate you after a long sleep.

Breakfast (8:00 - 8:30 AM)

Health-First Tip: Use minimal salt and oil in all options.

  • 1 bowl of Oats porridge (approx 40g dry oats) with fruits/nuts.
  • 1 plate of Poha (use minimal oil, add lots of vegetables).
  • 1 bowl of Upma (semolina or millet) with vegetables.
  • 2-3 egg-white omelette with vegetables (no cheese).
  • 2 Moong Dal Cheela (pancakes) with mint chutney.

Why: A high-fiber, high-protein breakfast keeps you full, controls blood sugar, and (especially oats) actively helps lower cholesterol.

Mid-Morning Snack (11:00 AM)

  • 1 medium fruit (Apple, Orange, Pear, or 1 bowl Papaya).
  • OR a handful (10-12) of unsalted Almonds or Walnuts.

Why: Prevents you from getting ravenously hungry by lunch. Fruit provides fiber, and nuts provide healthy fats that are good for your heart (in moderation).

Lunch (1:00 - 1:30 PM)

Health-First Tip: Make the salad the biggest portion on your plate!

  • 2 small Multigrain Rotis (or 1 bowl Ragi Mudde).
  • 1 bowl Sabzi (mixed vegetables - cooked with minimal oil/salt).
  • 1 bowl Dal (lentils - no heavy tadka).
  • 1 large bowl of raw Salad (cucumber, tomato, onion, carrot).
  • (Optional: 1 small bowl of low-fat curd).

Why: A perfectly balanced meal. Fiber (roti, sabzi, salad) and Protein (dal) keep you full and help cholesterol. The salad provides volume with few calories.

Evening Snack (4:00 - 4:30 PM)

  • 1 cup of Green Tea (no sugar).
  • WITH a handful of roasted Chana (unsalted).
  • OR 1 bowl of boiled Sprouts chaat (add lemon, no salt).

Why: This is the "danger zone" for unhealthy snacking. This protein/fiber snack prevents cravings and gives you energy for the evening.

Dinner (7:00 - 7:30 PM)
